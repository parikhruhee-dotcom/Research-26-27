## M2 — Strain conformational atlas
Computed real SASA (freesasa), DSSP secondary structure (mkdssp 4.6.1),
inter-protofilament contact residues, per-strain PHF6/PHF6* in-fibril-context
burial, pairwise Kabsch RMSD + dendrogram on the shared core (306-378, 73
residues), and the AD strain fingerprint.

Notable finding/validation: initially computed the PHF6*-vs-PHF6
"steric-zipper burial" using the disease-fibril context itself and got a
ratio near 1.0 (~0.93-1.14) across the five dual-core folds — this did NOT
match the literature's "VQIINK buries ~2x VQIVYK" claim, because a fibril's
cross-beta packing and a hexapeptide's own steric-zipper dry interface are
related but different physical quantities. Fixed by computing burial on the
actual standalone hexapeptide zipper microcrystal structures instead (2ON9 =
VQIVYK, Sawaya lineage; 5V5C = VQIINK, microED), using RCSB's own
precomputed crystallographic assembly (symmetry mates already generated by
RCSB's pipeline, not hand-rolled space-group math) for a real dry-interface
burial calculation. Result: VQIINK/VQIVYK buried-SASA ratio = **2.031** —
matches the literature claim almost exactly. This is kept as a clearly
distinct computation from the per-strain in-fibril-context numbers (both are
reported, neither is deleted, both are labeled for what they actually
measure).

Also caught and fixed: the AD strain-fingerprint's #1-ranked hotspot
(Val306) was an artifact of the AD single-chain model's own truncated
N-terminus (elevated apparent SASA at a cut end, not real fold geometry) —
excluded the 3 residues nearest each boundary of the AD chain's own modeled
span from the AD side of the fingerprint ranking (not from the "other folds"
side, where those same positions are usually interior residues).
